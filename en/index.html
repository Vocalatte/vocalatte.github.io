WHO WE ARE
 
We were formed as a group of friends from university in Bratislava, Slovakia, who share a common passion for music and singing, we started to meet regularly until in 2011 we created the vocal-instrumental group (choir) Vocalatt√©, currently with 28 members. We sing four-part (SATB) arrangements of modern and classical compositions, a capella or with the accompaniment of piano, violin, flute and other musical instruments. Our public performances began with singing at exhibition openings, later continued with participation in charity concerts, weddings, university events as well as regular own performances. Our goal is primarily to spread the joy of singing that we have :)

REPERTOIRE
 
We sing whatever we like - from classical music, through sacred songs, film music, folk songs to contemporary popular music. We've been cooperating with the Rectorate of Comenius University and its Faculty of Mathematics, Physics and Informatics for a long time, and we've organized several events for both the private and public sectors. We've sung at our members' weddings (and not only theirs) - hence, we can playfully cover the wedding ceremony with singing and instrumental music. We do not object to cooperation with bands, on the contrary - we enjoyed a thematic concert in the style of jazz and swing, a concert dedicated to Queen or an Inclusion concert full of film and harder rock music, which we enjoyed a lot thanks to this collaboration. Of the older projects, the musical adaptation of Jesus Christ Superstar, a concert of songs by the Swedish band ABBA or a concert of soundtracks from Disney movies are certainly worth mentioning.
We like bringing joy through singing and sing for a higher good - this is how we have already pleased the Children's Hope Club, Amazons NGO, and even donated the revenue from a Christmas concert to the project Warmth and Light for Ukraine. In general, we probably enjoy the Christmas season the most, the many Christmas celebrations and Christmas concerts are the best proof. Apparently, thanks to the variety of our repertoire, we still maintain our enthusiasm for singing üôÇ
